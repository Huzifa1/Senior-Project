Time Out

It's not just a prison!

Time out is to take a breath, regroup and to evaluate your social life
NOT to PUNISH!

The way to reform
has always led through
prison.

TIME OUT PRISON is a typical modern prison which has been built since June, 2021 whose ranked as the most humanly prison in the world. It is a place where the prisoner develops his important psychological values and needs to return and reintegrate into the society again through government-contracted psychologists and social workers. The inmates of this prison are classified as the lowest criminal and aggressive in the country, their crime varies between unintentional murder, fraud, administrative corruption, etc..
However, TIME OUT PRISON is characterized by an advanced system through which officers can easily control everything in the prison, also the prisoner's acquaintances can handle a date online through the website to meet his imprisoned relative.

This jail give the prisoners more rights than other Prisons, here are some of their rights:

  • They can use smart phones in a limited way, so they can read their favorite books and follow their daily schedule.
  • They have various gymnasiums and playgrounds, thus they can unload their energies and improve their health.
  • They can learn and work in many handmade crafts, therefore the prisoners will have the ability to sell their items in this website and collect money.

General Visiting Information!

Make sure your visit will be a success by carefully following these rules:

Visitor identification

Immediate family, relatives and other types of approved visitors (friends/associates, Members of religious groups and lawyers).

Dress Code

Wearing inappropriate clothing (such as provocative or revealing clothes) may result in your being denied visitation.

Physical Contact

In most cases, handshakes, hugs are allowed in the visit. Staff may limit contact for security reasons and to keep the visiting area orderly.

Visiting Duration

By law, an inmate gets at least four hours of visiting time per month. However, the Warden can restrict the length of visits to avoid overcrowding in the visiting room.

General Behavior

Because many people are usually visiting, it is important visits are quiet, orderly, and dignified. The visiting room officer can require you to leave if either you or the inmate is not acting appropriately.

Visiting Schedules

Weekends are the most popular time to visit so prisons may choose to limit visits to either Saturday or Sunday and the day will vary for each inmate. The inmate you plan to visit should tell you his needs and the visiting schedule for the prison.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can you connect with your relatives in the prison?

Inmates are allowed to make outgoing calls only, and under no circumstances are incoming calls allowed,but they can use pre-paid phone accounts setup through third party vendors.

What happens if a prisoner shows criminal behaviour?

When a prisoner creates problems and repeatedly disobeys orders or appears criminal, we transfer him to another prison designated for this type of prisoner.
take out prison is specified for prisoners classified as non-criminals.

How do prisoners typically spend their days?

Prisoners' daily life takes place according to a daily schedule. This will prescribe the wake-up, roll-calls, morning exercises, times for meals, times for studying and working, as well as the times prescribed for sports events and walks.
Check the daily routine of a prisoner from his point of view by clicking here.

How is food served?

Prison food is made by prisoners assigned to Food Service. These prisoners both cook and serve the food, though they do so under the supervision of prison guards who have a background in food service. As a prisoner, you don't have a choice in the food you're served.

What if staff is treating the inmate unfairly?

As stated in the TDCJ Offender Orientation Handbook, given to each inmate upon entry into the prison system, inmates have the right to file grievances regarding their treatment at the facilities and units. According to policy, inmate grievances must first be submitted to the warden or unit grievance staff. If the inmate is not satisfied with the warden or unit grievance staff's decision, the inmate may then file the next level of grievance, as stated in the TDCJ Offender Orientation Handbook (English or Frensh).

How can you transfer money to your relatives in the prison?

You can transfer money to the accounting office of TAKE OUT prison
IBAN number: CA 11 2201 0320 0002 6563 02
In the reference field, enter the first name and the last name of the prisoner and - if known - the prisoner ID number. If you do not know this, the date of birth is sufficient.
These deposits are possible a maximum of 12 times a year with a total amount of 3840 dollars! Cash deposits cannot be accepted in the TAKE OUT prison!
